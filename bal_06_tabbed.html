Final Hypothesis
Unified Contrastive Alignment with Provable Cross-lingual Transfer
We propose UCA, a training framework that jointly optimizes contrastive alignment across parallel and comparable corpora using curriculum-scheduled difficulty. We prove that UCA converges to a shared representation subspace under mild distributional assumptions, and derive zero-shot transfer bounds for unseen languages based on typological distance. Our framework achieves state-of-the-art on XTREME and Tatoeba benchmarks while requiring 3x less parallel data than existing methods.
