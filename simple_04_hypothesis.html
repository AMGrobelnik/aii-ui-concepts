Final Hypothesis

Cross-lingual Contrastive Pre-training with Synthetic Code-Switching for Low-Resource Transfer

We propose a novel pre-training framework that combines synthetically generated code-switched text with a contrastive alignment objective to improve cross-lingual transfer to low-resource languages. Unlike existing approaches that rely on parallel corpora or machine translation, our method generates training signal by algorithmically interleaving tokens from high-resource and target languages at linguistically motivated boundaries. A contrastive loss then aligns the resulting representations across language pairs, enabling strong zero-shot performance on downstream tasks including NER, sentiment analysis, and question answering across 12 typologically diverse language pairs.

Key terms Code-Switching Augmentation Contrastive Alignment Low-Resource NLP
Significance

This approach eliminates the dependency on parallel corpora for cross-lingual transfer, opening a practical path to high-quality NLP for the hundreds of languages that lack sufficient translation data. The contrastive framing also provides a principled mechanism for analyzing representation alignment quality.
