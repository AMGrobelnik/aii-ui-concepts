Title: Cross-lingual Contrastive Pre-training with Synthetic Code-Switching for Low-Resource Transfer

Abstract: We propose augmenting multilingual pre-training with a contrastive objective over synthetically code-switched sentence pairs. By generating code-switched variants through token-level lexicon substitution and phrase-level alignment swaps, we create hard positive pairs that force the encoder to learn language-invariant representations. Experiments on 8 low-resource languages show +3.2 F1 over XLM-R and +1.8 F1 over translate-train on NER and sentiment tasks.

Review: Well-scoped hypothesis with clear mechanism, measurable claims, and controlled evaluation design. Ready for invention.
