Cross-lingual contrastive pre-training with synthetic code-switching improves zero-shot transfer by 15–23% on low-resource languages

We propose a contrastive pre-training framework that leverages synthetic code-switching to create cross-lingual alignment signals. By training on artificially code-switched parallel corpora with an InfoNCE objective, our method achieves 15–23% relative improvement on zero-shot cross-lingual transfer benchmarks for Yoruba, Swahili, and Quechua.
