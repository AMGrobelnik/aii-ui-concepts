Abstract

We present a novel cross-lingual pre-training framework that leverages synthetic code-switching to improve zero-shot transfer to low-resource languages. Our approach combines contrastive learning objectives with linguistically-motivated code-switching strategies, enabling the model to learn robust cross-lingual representations without parallel data. Experiments across 20 languages on the XTREME benchmark demonstrate consistent improvements of 3.2–7.8 F1 points over strong baselines, with particularly notable gains for typologically distant language pairs.

1  Introduction

Cross-lingual transfer learning has emerged as a promising paradigm for extending NLP capabilities to low-resource languages. However, existing approaches often rely on parallel corpora or translation systems that are unavailable for many language pairs.
