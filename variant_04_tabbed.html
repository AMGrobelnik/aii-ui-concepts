Self-Supervised Contrastive Pre-Training for Low-Resource NER with Adaptive Negative Sampling
Anonymous Authors · Under Review · 2026
Abstract — We propose a novel self-supervised contrastive pre-training framework tailored for named entity recognition in low-resource language settings. Our approach introduces an adaptive negative sampling strategy that dynamically adjusts the difficulty of negative examples during contrastive learning, enabling the model to learn more discriminative entity representations. Experiments on five low-resource languages demonstrate consistent improvements of 3.2–5.8 F1 points over strong baselines.
1. Introduction

Named entity recognition (NER) remains a cornerstone task in information extraction, yet its reliance on large annotated corpora limits applicability to low-resource languages. Recent advances in self-supervised pre-training have shown promise for transfer learning, but standard approaches often fail to capture entity-specific representations needed for fine-grained NER.

2. Related Work

Contrastive learning has emerged as a powerful paradigm for representation learning across domains. In NLP, approaches such as SimCSE and DeCLUTR have demonstrated the effectiveness of contrastive objectives for sentence-level representations. However, token-level contrastive objectives for NER remain underexplored, particularly in cross-lingual settings.

3. Method

Our framework consists of two key components: (i) a token-level contrastive pre-training objective with curriculum-based negative sampling, and (ii) a cross-lingual alignment module that leverages parallel entity mentions across languages to learn shared entity representations.
